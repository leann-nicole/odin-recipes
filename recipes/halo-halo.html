Halo-Halo

Description

Halo-halo is a famous dessert in the Philippines. It is composed of various ingredients that are all mixed together, along with shaved ice and evaporated milk.

Some of he common ingredient used to make Halo-halo are sugar palm (kaong), boiled kidney beans, flat rice crisps (pinipig), coconut gel (nata de coco), ripe jackfruit, colored gelatin, tapioca pearls, sweetened plantains, and macapuno.

The ingredients are placed in a tall glass with a few teaspoons of sugar. Shaved ice is added after all the components are in place. Special Halo-halo is topped with leche flan and ube halaya â€” sometimes ice cream is added. Evaporated milk is poured on top for the finale.

Ingredients

  • sugar palm (kaong)
  • boiled kidney beans
  • flat rice crisps (pinipig)
  • coconut gel (nata de coco)
  • ripe jackfruit
  • colored gelatin
  • tapioca pearls
  • sweetened plantains
  • macapuno
  • evaporated milk

Steps

  1. Place ingredients in a tall glass with a few teaspoons of sugar.
  2. Add shaved ice.
  3. Optionally, top with leche flan, ube halaya, and ice cream.
  4. Pour evaporated milk on top for the finale.
